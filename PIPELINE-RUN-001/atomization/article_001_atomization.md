## TITLE: What happens to my Florida ACA subsidy in 2026? A Fiduciary Breakdown.

I see a lot of anxiety here regarding the "2026 subsidy cliff" for marketplace plans in Florida, and for good reason—Florida has the highest enrollment in the nation. As it stands, the enhanced premium tax credits from the American Rescue Plan are scheduled to expire on December 31, 2025.

If your household income is above the 400% Federal Poverty Level (approximately $81k for a family of two, or $103k for a family of three), your current subsidy is likely to vanish overnight on January 1, 2026. This is the "cliff." Passing that income line by even $1 can result in your monthly premium doubling or tripling.

**Key things for Florida residents to watch:**
1. **MAGI Management:** While HSAs and tax-loss harvesting can help reduce your Adjusted Gross Income, for many self-employed professionals, the gap is too large for these simple "hacks" to work.
2. **The 400% FPL Cap:** This hard cap returns in 2026 unless Congress passes a new extension.
3. **Plan Quality:** We're seeing a trend of "Silver" plans essentially becoming as expensive as "Gold" plans once the subsidies are removed.

For those of you in that early retiree or self-employed bracket ($80k-$150k income range), this is a moment for a "Structural Decision" rather than just plan-shopping. Look into "Premium Reallocation" strategies—where you pivot that redirected premium cost into private wealth vehicles you actually own, rather than renting a government-managed benefit.

I recommend running a 2026 MAGI projection now rather than waiting for the November open enrollment period.
